PROPOSAL
--------
Each run starts from the published paper and rebuilds the work on its own: it downloads
the real models and the real task data, re-creates the paper's exact method and settings,
trains the two smallest models for the full schedule on a single GPU, and then grades the
result against a detailed checklist that both reads the code and confirms the model
actually trained.

We ask for five runs because that is the smallest number that tells a solid reproduction
apart from a lucky one. It also covers the runs that fail and have to retry (the hardest
run so far needed 11 tries), and it gives us real measured costs instead of estimates.

Every run has two costs: the AI models that read the paper and write the code, and the
GPU time that is needed for the paper. The key result: the AI-model cost is small no matter
which model we use, while GPU time is what really drives the total. Five full runs cost
about $24 on our recommended model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) and at most $79 on the priciest
option (Table 1). GPU time is the real lever: five runs cost $60-120 on a basic card,
about $90-175 on a mid card, or $340-680 on a high-end card (Table 2). We request about
$1,000, enough for five high-end-card runs plus a 20% cushion.
